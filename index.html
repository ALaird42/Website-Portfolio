About Me

I graduated from the University of Illinois U-C in 2017, with a degree in Music Education. I taught classroom music during the 2017-2018 school year. Since then I have bounced around to many areas of the classical music industry, including musical instrument repair, retail, and teaching private lessons (which I still do). I find the tailoring of my teaching to my individual students to be fun, rewarding, and a better application of my skills as an educator.

I decided to make the change into software during the COVID-19 pandemic when the entertainment industry was hit particularly hard by the lockdowns and social distancing restrictions. While I still love and enjoy musical performance and teaching, I decided to move away from it as a career, and into something more stable, engaging, and fulfilling. I belive working with code will give me the changes I am looking for, while still presenting fun and exciting new challenges and opportunites for personal growth.

I really enjoy working as a part of a team and getting to flex my creative muscles. Software development seemed like a no brainer for both of those!

Outside of work and teaching, I like to:

  • Watch movies (mostly action and comedy)
  • Play videogames
  • 3D print ridiculous props and goofy things
